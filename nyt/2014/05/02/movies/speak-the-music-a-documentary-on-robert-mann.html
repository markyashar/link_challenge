    A Life in a String Quartet, and Beyond

    ‘Speak the Music,’ a Documentary on Robert Mann

    Speak the Music: Robert Mann and the Mysteries of Chamber Music

    By NEIL GENZLINGERMAY 1, 2014

    Inside

      Allan Miller wisely keeps the hagiography to a minimum in “Speak the Music,” his documentary about Robert Mann, the longtime violinist for the Juilliard String Quartet. Though colleagues and former students chime in, Mr. Miller lets Mr. Mann and his violin do most of the talking, drawing on assorted interviews and vintage performance clips that convey both the skill and the enthusiasm underpinning his subject’s long career.

      Mr. Mann, now in his 90s, was a founder of the Juilliard Quartet in 1946 and played with the group for 51 years, retiring in 1997. He recalls one particular music lesson from his childhood in Oregon that opened his eyes.

      “Up to then, I was going to be a forest ranger, hopefully in a national park,” he says. “That was my dream. But that day, I thought, ‘You know, music is very interesting.’ ”

      He learned not only to play extremely well but also to study the music and the composer to find the meaning and intent beneath the notes. The film is subtitled “Robert Mann and the Mysteries of Chamber Music,” and it includes scenes of him teaching and coaching young players, trying to convey nuances that, frankly, might be accessible only to viewers who are musicians, too.

      But Mr. Mann also speaks movingly of having to be a student of sorts himself, well into his career: He was with the quartet for so long that other players cycled in and out, bringing fresh perspectives to which he had to adjust.
