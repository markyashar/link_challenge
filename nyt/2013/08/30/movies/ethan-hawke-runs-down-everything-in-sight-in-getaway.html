    Want to See the Wife Again? Just Follow Directions

    Ethan Hawke Runs Down Everything in Sight in ‘Getaway’

    Getaway

    By STEPHEN HOLDENAUG. 29, 2013

    Inside

      Continue reading the main story

      One can only guess why Ethan Hawke felt compelled to make a high dive from the sublimity of “Before Midnight” into the twisted rubble of “Getaway.” What other reason could there be for a star to attach his name to a mindless demolition derby but the payday? Furthermore, Mr. Hawke is only the nominal star of a movie whose driving force is the ultimate muscle car — an armored Ford Shelby GT500 Super Snake — that runs amok in the streets of Sofia, Bulgaria.

      Mr. Hawke’s character, Brent Magna, is a former professional racecar driver whose wife (Rebecca Budig) is kidnapped. An anonymous caller, identified in the credits as the Voice (Jon Voight), warns Brent that he will never see her again unless he steals a vehicle from a parking garage and follows instructions.

      For the next 80 minutes, Brent is forced to act out an adolescent sociopath’s secret dream of running down everything in sight while driving 100 miles an hour in the wrong direction, destroying several fleets of police cars and mowing through who knows how many pedestrians. Just when the vicarious thrill of watching him obey the Voice’s order to “smash everything you can” loses its novelty, Brent is carjacked by the Kid (Selena Gomez), a shrill, bossy young woman with a baby face and a chip on her shoulder.

      It seems that the vehicle was her car, stolen, then souped-up for the big chase. As her tantrum subsides, we learn that the car was a present from her father, the chief executive of the city’s largest investment bank. She is also a computer whiz awkwardly spouting reams of techno jargon, spiced with profanities, as she helps Brent outwit the Voice.

      Directed by Courtney Solomon from a screenplay by Sean Finegan and Gregg Maxwell Parker, “Getaway” was constructed in the editing room. The glimmers of wit and carnival humor in the “Fast & Furious” franchise are nowhere to be found in “Getaway,” except in its bizarre note of seasonal cheer. It’s Christmastime, and every now and then a fragment of “Jingle Bell Rock” interrupts the generic pumped-up soundtrack.

      “Getaway” is rated PG-13 (Parents strongly cautioned). It has intense action, rude gestures and strong language.

      Getaway

      Opens on Friday.

      Directed by Courtney Solomon; written by Sean Finegan and Gregg Maxwell Parker; director of photography, Yaron Levy; edited by Ryan Dufrene; music by Justin Burnett; production design by Nate Jones; costumes by Roseanne Fiedler; produced by Mr. Solomon, Allan Zeman, Moshe Diamant and Christopher Milburn; released by Warner Brothers Pictures. Running time: 1 hour 30 minutes.

      WITH: Ethan Hawke (Brent Magna), Selena Gomez (the Kid), Jon Voight (the Voice) and Rebecca Budig (Leanne).
